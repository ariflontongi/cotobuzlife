What To Do In San Antonio Events

Fiesta FOMO Got You Down? Fear Not, Fun Seekers! Your Guide to San Antonio's Sizzling Events

So, you missed out on the bedazzled boots and boozy breakfasts of Fiesta. Don't fret, friend, because San Antonio is a city that never lets the party die! Here's your one-stop guide to keep your calendar chock-full of epic events, from rodeos that'll rock your boots to museum exhibits that'll blow your mind (hopefully not literally).

Historian in the Making? Dive Deep into the Alamo City's Past

  • Step Back in Time at the Alamo: Brush up on your Texas history (or pretend you know more than you do) with a visit to the Alamo. This iconic landmark isn't just about "Remember the Alamo!" – it's a fascinating look at the fight for Texan independence. Just be sure to channel your inner William B. Travis and resist the urge to yell obscenities at the gift shop prices.
  • **Explore the Missions: **San Antonio's Spanish colonial missions are a UNESCO World Heritage Site, and for good reason. These historic gems offer a glimpse into the city's past, with beautiful architecture and peaceful courtyards that are perfect for escaping the summer heat (and maybe a rogue mariachi band).

Calling All Culture Vultures!

  • Feast Your Eyes (and Maybe Your Stomach) at the Briscoe Western Art Museum: Saddle up for a journey through the Wild West at the Briscoe. From iconic paintings to stunning sculptures, this museum showcases the art, culture, and history of the American West. Bonus points if you can identify all the different types of cacti in the gift shop (because who doesn't need a prickly pear phone case?)
  • Go Beyond the Obvious at the McNay Art Museum: Sure, the Alamo is cool, but have you seen the McNay? This museum boasts an impressive collection of modern and contemporary art, with everything from Van Gogh to Frida Kahlo. Even if modern art makes you go "hmm," the McNay's beautiful grounds are worth a visit for some artsy Instagram pics.

Need a Break from the Museums? Let's Get Physical!

  • Saddle Up for a Night at the San Antonio Stock Show & Rodeo: Yeehaw! The rodeo isn't just for cowboys anymore (unless by "cowboy" you mean someone who wears a bolo tie ironically). Witness heart-stopping bull riding, cheer on your favorite mutton busters (yes, that's a real thing), and indulge in some good ol' fashioned Texas cuisine.
  • Get Your Thrill On at Six Flags Fiesta Texas: Feeling the need for speed? Six Flags Fiesta Texas has you covered. From scream-inducing rollercoasters to laugh-out-loud water rides, this theme park is guaranteed to give you a day full of thrills (and maybe a churro-induced sugar rush).

How to Avoid Becoming a Hot Mess (Literally) at a San Antonio Event

  • Hydration is Key: San Antonio summers are no joke. Bring a reusable water bottle and refill it often. Drinking local craft beer is great, but water is your BFF.
  • Sunscreen is Your Superpower: Slap on that sunscreen! SPF 50 is your friend, and don't forget those sunglasses and a hat.
  • Embrace the Two-Step: You might get caught in a spontaneous line dance at a rodeo or a two-step at a honky-tonk. Don't worry, nobody expects you to be a pro, just have fun with it!

Bonus FAQ for the Super Enthusiastic Tourist

How to get around San Antonio? The city has a decent bus system, but rideshare apps like Uber and Lyft are your best bet for getting around efficiently.What to wear? San Antonio is a casual city. Pack comfortable shoes for all the walking you'll be doing, and don't forget your swimsuit for those inevitable hotel pool dips.What to eat? Tex-Mex is a must, but don't miss out on the city's amazing barbacoa, breakfast tacos, and all things BBQ.
