When it comes to outboard motors, what is the lower unit?

In an outboard motor, the lower unit (also known as the “foot”) is the bottom part that contains the propeller and gear case. Preserving the condition of your lower unit is critical to ensuring that your outboard runs smoothly and has a long service life.

What is the operation of an outboard lower unit?

The lower unit (also known as the gearcase) of an outboard motor is the component of the outboard that receives power and rotation from the engine and transfers it to the prop shaft and propeller. By doing so, your boat’s propeller will begin to rotate, propelling it forward through the water.
