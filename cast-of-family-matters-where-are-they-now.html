Cast Of Family Matters Where Are They Now?

Publish date: 2024-08-12

The 1989 sitcom “Family Matters” became a staple of American television, running for nine seasons and leaving a lasting impression on its audience. The show centered around the Winslow family and their nerdy neighbor Steve Urkel, played by Jaleel White. Over the years, fans have often wondered what happened to the beloved cast members after the show ended. This article will explore where the cast of “Family Matters” is now, providing a comprehensive update on their lives and careers post-show.

Reginald VelJohnson (Carl Winslow)

Reginald VelJohnson portrayed the patriarch of the Winslow family, Carl Winslow, a role that made him a household name. Since the show’s conclusion, VelJohnson has continued acting, with appearances in TV shows like “Hart of Dixie,” “Mike & Molly,” and “Brooklyn Nine-Nine.” He has also done voice work for animated series and has been involved in stage productions.

Jo Marie Payton (Harriette Winslow)

Jo Marie Payton, who played the Winslow family matriarch Harriette Winslow, left “Family Matters” during its final season but didn’t slow down in her career. She has appeared in numerous television shows, including “The Proud Family” as the voice of Suga Mama, and has had guest roles on shows like “Will & Grace” and “Mann & Wife.”

Jaleel White (Steve Urkel)

Jaleel White’s portrayal of Steve Urkel made him an iconic character in TV history. After “Family Matters,” White continued acting, with roles in “Grown Ups” and “Me, Myself & I.” He also participated in “Dancing with the Stars” and has been involved in web series and voice acting, including voicing the character Sonic the Hedgehog in various animated series.

Kellie Shanygne Williams (Laura Winslow)

Kellie Shanygne Williams played Laura Winslow, the object of Urkel’s affection. Post-“Family Matters,” Williams has had a quieter career, focusing on her family and occasional acting roles. She founded the Kellie Williams Program, which helps Washington, D.C. students produce a television show.

Darius McCrary (Eddie Winslow)

Darius McCrary took on the role of Eddie Winslow, the eldest Winslow child. Since the show ended, McCrary has appeared in various television series, including “The Young and the Restless” and “Star.” He has also pursued a music career and has been involved in film projects.

Bryton James (Richie Crawford)

Bryton James, who played Richie Crawford, Harriette’s nephew, transitioned to soap operas after “Family Matters,” most notably “The Young and the Restless,” for which he has won multiple Daytime Emmy Awards. He also voices characters in animated series and video games.

Rosetta LeNoire (Mother Winslow)

Rosetta LeNoire, who played the lovable Mother Winslow, continued acting until her health declined. She passed away in 2002, leaving behind a legacy in theater and television.

Telma Hopkins (Rachel Crawford)

Telma Hopkins, who portrayed Rachel Crawford, Harriette’s sister, has had a successful career in television, with roles in “Half & Half,” “Are We There Yet?” and a recurring role in “Dead to Me.” She also continues to sing, having been part of the 1970s music group Tony Orlando and Dawn.

Jaimee Foxworth (Judy Winslow)

Jaimee Foxworth, who played the youngest Winslow daughter Judy, faced personal struggles after her character was written off the show. She has since worked on rebuilding her life and appeared in “Celebrity Rehab with Dr. Drew” to address her issues.

Shawn Harrison (Waldo Geraldo Faldo)

Shawn Harrison amused audiences as Waldo Geraldo Faldo, Eddie’s best friend. Post-show, Harrison has done voice acting, including work on the animated series “Legion of Super Heroes,” and has made guest appearances on television shows.

Michelle Thomas (Myra Monkhouse)

Michelle Thomas played Myra Monkhouse, Steve Urkel’s girlfriend. Tragically, Thomas passed away from stomach cancer in 1998, shortly after her time on “Family Matters” ended.

Orlando Brown (3J Winslow)

Orlando Brown appeared in later seasons as 3J Winslow, an adopted member of the Winslow family. Brown has faced legal and personal issues in recent years but has also continued acting and making music.
